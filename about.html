About Us

Who We Are

Atlantic Tutoring brings world-class, curriculum-spanning education to students across the globe. Between us, we bring over two decades of international teaching experience and high-level industry practice. We know what academic excellence looks like at the highest levels, and we know how to help students get there. Deep expertise, however, is only half the equation. Whether we are breaking down A-Level Maths or helping a student navigate IB Global Politics, our approach is rooted in patience and empathy. We have both experienced the challenges of rigorous academia firsthand. We teach students how to think critically, find their voice, and rediscover the genuine joy of learning.

Hannah Coleman

Maths · Computer Science · Physics

Hannah knows what it's like to struggle with maths. She got an E in her GCSE — the kind of grade that makes you think maths just isn't for you. But she didn't stop there. She went on to earn a BSc in Physics from the University of Nottingham, research a PhD in Medical Physics and Machine Learning at UCL's Centre for Advanced Biomedical Imaging, and build a career as a Quantitative Risk Analyst in London, writing Python code and building risk models for an asset management firm.

That journey — from 'I can't do maths' to using it every day at the highest level — is what shapes how she teaches. She's not the tutor who finds everything easy and can't understand why you don't get it. She's the one who figured out how to get it, step by step, and can show you how to do the same.

Hannah specialises in GCSE and MYP Mathematics, AS-Level and IB Mathematics, IB Computer Science, Internal Assessment support for Maths and CS, Python programming fundamentals, and Physics foundations.

She's also a published science communicator (Physics World) and has spent years doing outreach at events including the Royal Society Summer Science Festival, Cheltenham Science Festival, and New Scientist Live — so explaining complex ideas in accessible ways is second nature.

BA East European Civilisations — University of Nottingham

PhD Researcher, Medical Physics & Machine Learning — UCL

Cameron MacDonald

English · Global Politics · Theatre · TOK

Cameron is an educator with more than 20 years of classroom experience across four countries. He's taught in Canada, the United States, Australia, and the United Kingdom, bringing a genuinely global perspective to his work with students.

Most recently, Cameron spent over seven years at EIFA International School in London — a bilingual French/English school — where he was part of the team that established IB World School accreditation. He coordinated Theory of Knowledge across the school, taught IB Global Politics and Theatre, and guided students through Extended Essays in multiple subjects. Before that, he spent eight years teaching English and Theatre Studies at St Monica's College in Melbourne.

Cameron's approach is pastoral and student-centred. He believes that academic success starts with feeling supported, and he has a talent for meeting students where they are and helping them find their voice — whether that's in an essay, a TOK presentation, or a Global Politics exam.

He is currently completing a Master of Education at St. Francis Xavier University, with a focus on Educational Administration and Policy.

Cameron specialises in IB English (Language & Literature / Literature), IB Global Politics, IB Theatre, Theory of Knowledge (TOK), Extended Essay supervision and guidance, iGCSE Drama and English, and Cambridge Lower Secondary English.

MEd (in progress) — St. Francis Xavier University

BEd — University of British Columbia

20+ years teaching experience across Canada, USA, Australia, and UK
